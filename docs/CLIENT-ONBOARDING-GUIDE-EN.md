## Your Dashboard

### Overview Page
Shows your bot's performance at a glance:
- **Bot Status** — Green = your bot is active. Yellow = paused. You can toggle it on/off.
- **Conversations this month** — How many customer chats were handled
- **Messages handled** — Total messages sent by your bot
- **Escalations** — Conversations that need your personal follow-up

### Conversations
View every conversation your bot has had. You can:
- Search by keyword
- Filter by channel (WhatsApp / Instagram) or status
- Click any conversation to read the full chat

### Escalations Inbox
When a customer is upset, asks for a refund, or asks something your bot can't answer,
the conversation is flagged here. **Check this daily.**
Click "Resolve" after you've personally followed up with the customer.

### Analytics
Charts showing message volume, peak hours, and language breakdown.
Data updates daily.

### Settings
Edit your bot's configuration:
- **Services & Pricing** — Update whenever your prices change
- **Business Hours** — Your bot always tells customers the correct hours
- **Bot Language** — Auto-detect (recommended) or fixed language
- **Escalation Keywords** — Words that trigger human follow-up (e.g. "refund")
- **Pause Bot** — Use the toggle at the top to pause your bot during holidays

## What Your Bot Can and Cannot Do

**Can do:**
- Answer questions about your services and prices
- Tell customers your hours and location
- Handle questions in Hebrew, Russian, and English
- Direct customers to your booking link
- Escalate upset customers for your personal follow-up

**Cannot do:**
- Make appointments or take bookings directly (it directs to your booking link)
- Handle payments
- Handle images or voice messages (it asks customers to type)
- Make promises or commitments on your behalf

## Tips for Best Results

1. **Keep your services list updated** — If you add a new service, update it in Settings
2. **Check escalations daily** — Respond within your stated SLA time
3. **Review conversations weekly** — See what questions customers are asking most
